Phase 01

Business & Data Understanding

This phase ensures the technical effort aligns with real sector needs. The goal is for entities like MinCultura or IDPC to shift from costly corrective maintenance to preventive maintenance.

01
Business Objectives
Optimize state budget by shifting from corrective to preventive maintenance in Colombian cultural heritage assets.
02
Key Questions
Which material and PM10 combination accelerates deterioration? How does road proximity affect structures over 200 years old?
03
Data Evaluation
Dataset de 1.200 BIC records cruzando variables históricas, climáticas y urbanísticas de fuentes abiertas (IDEAM, OSM).
04
Feasibility Analysis
High technical feasibility with open data. Main risk: cadastral updates in small municipalities.
01
Selection
Integration of regional climate dictionaries (IDEAM) and BIC listings (MinCultura) as primary sources.
02
Cleaning
Strict rules: humidity ≤ 100%, age between 50–450 years, and zero null values across all 1,200 records.
03
Transformation
Feature Engineering (acid rain index), One-Hot Encoding for City and Material, and Min-Max / Standard scaling.
04
Documentation
Log of transformations, risk level calculation logic, and random seed for full reproducibility.

Phase 02

Data Engineering

Properly prepare data to maximize predictive potential and minimize bias risks. According to ML-Ops.org, all decisions must be documented to ensure reproducibility.

Phase 03

Model Engineering

Schmid et al. (2020) highlight the importance of incorporating domain expert knowledge in this phase. Detailed documentation facilitates future audits.

01
Selection de Algoritmos
Selecting appropriate techniques for multiclass classification based on the heritage dataset characteristics.
02
Training
Applying prepared data to the selected algorithm, respecting the random seed for reproducibility.
03
Hyperparameter Tuning
Optimizing configurations to improve predictive performance across the 3 deterioration levels.
04
Validation
Verifying performance with unseen training data to ensure generalization capability.
01
Performance Metrics
Accuracy, weighted F1-score, confusion matrix, and AUC-ROC for multiclass classification evaluation.
02
Validation Cruzada
K-Fold Cross Validation to evaluate model generalization with different dataset subsets.
03
Risk Analysis
Identificación de sobreajuste y sesgos, especialmente por el desbalance de clases (High: solo 1.5%).
04
Expert Review
Validation con especialistas en conservación patrimonial para asegurar la relevancia práctica del modelo.

Phase 04

Model Evaluation

Según KDnuggets (2025), rigorous evaluation is essential to ensure reliability in real-world environments. This phase determines if the model is ready for production.

Phase 05

Model Deployment

ML-Ops.org highlights that robust mechanisms for feedback collection and incident detection are essential in the production system.

01
Implementation
Integration of the validated model into the real operational environment, compatible with existing systems.
02
Documentation
Technical and user manuals, with training for staff who will interact with the system.
03
Protocols
Continuous monitoring procedures, incident management, and feedback collection.
01
Continuous Monitoring
Regular monitoring of production performance, verifying expected precision levels with new data.
02
Degradation Detection
Early identification of "drift" or performance degradation due to changes in environmental data.
03
Model Update
Periodic retraining with new BIC data or parameter adjustments to maintain effectiveness.
04
Risk Review
Continuous vulnerability assessment and quality control adaptation as context evolves.

Phase 06

Monitoring & Maintenance

Schmid et al. (2020) emphasize that this is a continuous phase aimed at ensuring system quality over time, guaranteeing sustainable value.
