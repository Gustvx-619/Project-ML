Evaluation Methodology

This project solves a multiclass classification problem — predicting one of three deterioration levels: Low, Medium, or High. Therefore, classification metrics are applied. Regression metrics (MAE, MSE, RMSE, R²) are not applicable since there is no continuous numerical output.

Metrics used
• Accuracy — overall correct predictions
• Precision — quality of positive predictions
• Recall — coverage of actual positives
• F1-Score — harmonic mean of P & R
• AUC-ROC — discrimination ability
• Confusion Matrix — prediction breakdown
Evaluation setup
• Test set: 240 records (20% of dataset)
• Stratified split preserves class ratio
• Weighted average for imbalanced classes
• AUC: One-vs-Rest (OvR) strategy
• Random seed: 42 (reproducible)

08

Conclusions

The evaluation of the three models confirms that Random Forest is the optimal choice for predicting structural deterioration in Colombian Cultural Heritage Assets. With an accuracy of 88.33% and an AUC-ROC of 0.9571, it demonstrates both high classification accuracy and excellent discriminative power across all three deterioration classes.

The primary limitation across all models is the High class detection — with only 18 High records in the full dataset (1.5%), the test set contains just 3 High cases, making it statistically insufficient to properly evaluate this class. Future work should focus on collecting more High deterioration cases or applying SMOTE oversampling to address this imbalance.

Despite this limitation, the model provides significant value for preventive maintenance planning: correctly identifying 92.31% of Low-risk buildings and 85.11% of Medium-risk buildings allows MinCultura and IDPC to efficiently allocate conservation budgets and prioritize at-risk heritage assets before structural failure occurs.
