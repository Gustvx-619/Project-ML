Phase 1 · CRISP-ML

Business Understanding

Business and data understanding: aligning the technical effort with the real needs of the Colombian heritage sector.

01

Problem Context

Colombia holds an architectural heritage of exceptional historical value: colonial churches, centuries-old haciendas, historic centers, and earthen buildings that shape the cultural identity of the country. These structures — catalogued as Bienes de Interés Cultural (BIC) — are exposed to progressive deterioration caused by the interaction of climatic, environmental, and urban factors.

The current maintenance model is corrective: action is taken only when damage is already visible and costly. Entities such as MinCultura and IDPC lack predictive tools to anticipate deterioration before it occurs, generating cost overruns and irreversible loss of heritage.

This project proposes applying Machine Learning techniques on open environmental and urban variables (IDEAM, OpenStreetMap, Datos Abiertos Colombia) to predict the deterioration level of each BIC and efficiently prioritize preventive interventions.

02

Business Objectives

The main purpose is to translate the needs of the heritage sector into concrete Machine Learning goals, enabling a shift from a reactive model to a predictive and preventive one.

  • OBJ 1Develop a classification model that predicts the deterioration level (Low / Medium / High) of any BIC from its environmental, climatic, and urban variables.
  • OBJ 2Reduce corrective maintenance costs through early identification of at-risk structures, enabling planned preventive interventions.
  • OBJ 3Provide MinCultura, IDPC, and municipalities with a data-driven prioritization tool for efficient allocation of heritage conservation budgets.
  • OBJ 4Document the complete process under CRISP-ML methodology to ensure reproducibility, auditability, and scalability to new regions or time periods.
  • OBJ 5Demonstrate the technical feasibility of using only open data sources as sufficient input for a predictive heritage conservation system.

03

Questions the Model Must Answer

Question 01
Which combination of material and PM10 accelerates deterioration toward a High state most rapidly?
Adobe and rammed earth are porous materials highly susceptible to water erosion. Combined with high PM10 levels (acid rain), the transition from Low to High can accelerate significantly. The model must identify these critical interaction thresholds.
Question 02
How does vibration from proximity to main roads affect buildings over 200 years old?
Centuries-old structures have a lower capacity to absorb mechanical vibrations. The Distancia_Via_Principal_m variable combined with Edad_Estructura_Anios captures this synergistic risk. The model must quantify how road proximity accelerates deterioration in ancient heritage.
Question 03
Which variables have the greatest predictive power over the deterioration level?
Understanding which variables contribute most to the model (feature importance) allows public entities to focus their data collection and monitoring efforts on the highest-impact predictive indicators.
Question 04
Is it possible to predict the deterioration level with sufficient accuracy using only open data?
Validating that IDEAM, OpenStreetMap, and Datos Abiertos Colombia sources are sufficient as predictive input — without costly physical inspections — is a central feasibility objective of this project.

04

Feasibility Analysis

Technical Feasibility
High. The 1,200-record dataset is sufficient to train supervised classification algorithms. scikit-learn, pandas, and Flask are open-source and freely available. Deployment on Render or similar services is free.
Data Feasibility
High. Variables come from open sources: IDEAM (climate), OpenStreetMap (roads), Datos Abiertos Colombia (BIC). No private or costly data required. The dataset is already structured and clean.
Economic Feasibility
High. Implementation costs are minimal (free cloud infrastructure). The return is significant: preventive maintenance costs up to 5× less than corrective maintenance in historic heritage.
Organizational Feasibility
Medium. Requires MinCultura, IDPC, and municipalities to adopt the model and update their registries regularly. The main risk is inconsistent update frequency between large cities and small municipalities.

05

Risks & Limitations

High
Class imbalance in the dataset
Only 1.5% of records correspond to High deterioration. This can bias the model toward always predicting Low or Medium. Mitigation: oversampling techniques (SMOTE) or class weight adjustment.
High
Uneven cadastral updates across municipalities
Small municipalities do not update their heritage records as frequently as major cities, generating outdated data that reduces model accuracy.
Medium
Synthetic / simulated dataset
The dataset simulates real data with plausible distributions. In real production, variable correlations may differ, requiring retraining with field-verified data.
Low
Limitations of available variables
Factors such as historical seismic events, prior interventions, or original construction quality are not captured in the dataset. Their future inclusion would improve model accuracy.
